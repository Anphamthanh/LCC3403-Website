Who is the CIA?

An Education Incubator

Students of Georgia Tech are smart and innovative, but they lack the experience and the tools to make changes. To become world class individuals, they have to live those experiences and develop those tools within a learning environment that encourages them to act on their good ideas. To build students in these ways, there are many projects that can be done to better Georgia Tech as a school that also offer students the opportunity to develop these talents. Some example projects include rebuilding the t-square website, a smartphone app for students to manage their classes, and a better system for providing real time locations for our school buses and trolleys. During a student’s time at Georgia Tech, there could easily be a moment that he or she realizes they have an implementable idea that redesigns on good infrastructure or effectively and efficiently solves a recognized problem in education. How can we make these ideas transform into possible projects for our students to implement? What can make these ideas become reality?

Investors

As Georgia Tech currently is, it is difficult for students to join together and work on big projects because of barriers such as time, authority, and funding. This is where the Committee for Innovation Advancement (CIA) will be useful. The CIA has the ability to answer these questions and make positive changes for education here at the institute in addition to possibly answering education issues worldwide.

For many great new innovators there are factors that restrict them from implementing their ideas. From simply not having the ability to see their ideas into real solutions or possible business ventures, to also lacking the physical, financial and educational resources to get started. They need the time to implement, coordinate and to test, evaluate as well as gain feedback from others. These commitments can be at odds with existing demands and expectations. For some students they may lack skillsets required for the field in which they wish to invest research. They may wish to test new concepts that require an initial financial investment, or physical resources. What occurs throughout this process are constant roadblocks between a great new idea and the classroom that needs it.

In the current reality, ideas fail, current methods become outdated, and our institute may fall behind in demanding STEM majors. It would benefit both the institute and the student to offer the resources and connections necessary to bridge the gaps between implementation and innovation, offer resources and connections necessary to see innovative concepts implemented in real world classrooms. Provide feedback to administrative staff that offer a clear and accurate measurement of education quality.
